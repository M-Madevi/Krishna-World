🌟 Krishna Janmashtami Celebration 🌟

✨ The Divine Birth of Lord Krishna ✨

Krishna Janmashtami, also known as Gokulashtami, is celebrated on the eighth day (Ashtami) of the Krishna Paksha in the month of Bhadrapada (August–September).

It marks the birth of Lord Krishna, the eighth avatar of Lord Vishnu, who incarnated to destroy evil (Adharma), defeat Kansa, and bring joy, love, and devotion into the world.

🎉 Celebrations Across India 🎉

- In Mathura (birthplace) and Vrindavan (childhood land), Janmashtami is celebrated with grandeur.
- Dahi Handi is performed especially in Maharashtra, where youth form pyramids to break butter pots tied high, symbolizing Krishna’s mischief.

🕛 Midnight Celebrations

Devotees fast and stay awake till midnight, the exact time of Krishna’s birth. At that moment, idols of Baby Krishna are bathed, dressed, placed in cradles and worshipped.

🛕 Symbolism of Janmashtami

- Flute: Symbol of divine music and love
- Peacock feather: Represents beauty, Krishna's charm
- Makhan (Butter): Joy, simplicity, and Krishna’s playful nature
- Cows: Devotion, purity, and Krishna's role as Govinda

🕉️ Spiritual Message

Janmashtami teaches us that whenever evil rises, the divine will take form to protect righteousness. Lord Krishna’s teachings from the Bhagavad Gita are timeless, guiding the world toward love, action, and truth.
